# Brand guide: Wendy Squibb

*Distilled from `knowledge/sample-listings/wendy-squibb__2026-05-22__SCBG-Rev1.docx` (Wendy Squibb Style Guide, Rev 1). The docx is the citation; this file is what every session reads first.*

## Voice identity

Warm, conversational, enthusiastic. Aspirational but approachable — Wendy pulls buyers in with feeling first, then backs it with specific detail. Personal, never corporate.

## Tone

- **Formal to casual:** leans casual (direct "we" invitations, light exclamations, contractions where natural).
- **Warm to professional:** warm first, with quiet professional polish in the structure underneath.
- **Aspirational to grounded:** aspirational opener, grounded specifics through the body, warm landing.

## Signature patterns

- **Emotive opener with ellipses.** Hook lines like *"When only the most spectacular home will do…"* — pull the reader in before any facts.
- **Sentence-length variation.** Mix short punchy lines (*"Light, bright and lovely."*) with longer descriptive sentences.
- **Power vocabulary.** Frequent use of: magnificent, gorgeous, fabulous, stunning, light, bright, lovely, neat and tidy, on trend, spectacular.
- **Warm, personal CTA.** *"We would love to show you through this home…"* — invitations are first-person plural and felt, not transactional.

## Hard nos (beyond the No-Robot Rulebook)

- "boasts" (in any form — "boasts a kitchen", "boasts three bedrooms")
- "well-appointed"
- "accommodation is well catered for"
- Any cliché real-estate filler that drains warmth out of a line.

## Listing structure preferences

- **Length:** 450–500 words for the body.
- **Paragraphs:** 6–7.
- **Sentences:** 18–20, with deliberate length variation.
- **The 8-step formula** (in order):
  1. **Hook / Headline** — emotive opener with ellipses.
  2. **Lifestyle Appeal** — intro paragraph that creates desire via sunshine, views, space, entertaining.
  3. **Kitchen Focus** — quality appliances, finishes, storage, pantry, "bells & whistles".
  4. **Bedrooms & Bathrooms** — style, spaciousness, on-trend design, sometimes Vogue/luxury references.
  5. **Extra Features** — sunrooms, rumpus rooms, sheds, heating/cooling, storage.
  6. **Outdoor & Gardens** — sunshine, entertaining decks, neat yards, roses, veggie gardens, established plantings.
  7. **Location Benefits** — beach, cafes, schools, walking tracks, convenience.
  8. **Closing CTA** — warm, personal invitation, with open-home reference where applicable.
