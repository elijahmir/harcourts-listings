# Brand guide: Kurt Knowles

*Distilled from `knowledge/sample-listings/kurt-knowles__2026-05-28__CopyProBrandGuide.docx` (Kurt Knowles CopyPro Brand Guide). The docx is the citation and holds his three gold-standard listings; this file is what every session reads first.*

## Voice identity

Warm, relaxed, polished and informative. Kurt sells the lifestyle and the feel of a home, but he backs every bit of it with the practical detail a buyer actually needs to make a decision. The balance is the whole point: enough atmosphere that a buyer can picture living there, never so much that the writing tips into fluff, hype or exaggeration. Calm and genuine, the same way clients describe him in person.

## Tone

- **Formal to casual:** relaxed and conversational, easy to read, but always professional. Never corporate, never sales-heavy.
- **Warm to professional:** warm and welcoming on top, real property knowledge underneath.
- **Aspirational to grounded:** lifestyle-driven opening, grounded practical detail through the body, a lifestyle-and-location landing. When atmosphere and substance pull against each other, substance wins.

## Signature patterns

- **Lifestyle-driven opener.** Lead with the feel and the setting — the calm, the privacy, the views, the slower pace — before working through the rooms. (Gold-standard openers: "Hidden behind a stunning brand new fence in a peaceful cul-de-sac setting…", "Lake Views, Fresh Air And Complete Peace At Tods Corner".)
- **Light and feel before features.** Describe the natural light and the overall atmosphere of the home early, then flow into kitchen, living and entertaining.
- **Practical payoff.** Every lifestyle line earns its place by landing on something concrete: stone benchtops, two heat pumps, double glazing, off-street parking for the boat and caravan, rainwater tanks, floor heating. Buyers should finish knowing how the home actually works.
- **Restraint over hype.** No over-the-top claims, no breathless emotion. "This home just works" and "buyers will absolutely love" are about as bold as it gets.
- **Warm, plain CTA.** Always closes with the exact line: "For more information or to book a private inspection please call the listing agent Kurt Knowles today!"

## Hard nos (beyond the No-Robot Rulebook)

- "boasts" (any form)
- "well appointed" / "well-appointed"
- "accommodation is well catered for"
- "cozy" (estate-agent code for small)
- "quaint" (code for old or dated)
- "fixer-upper"
- Corporate or overtly sales-heavy wording.
- Overly emotional, exaggerated or "over the top" descriptions. If a line feels unrealistic or like a stretch, cut it.

> **Source tension to be aware of:** the brand guide also lists "lovely" under words to avoid, yet all three gold-standard listings use it freely ("lovely sunshine", "this lovely home", "lovely sense of calm"). Treated here as use-sparingly rather than a hard ban, because it is plainly part of Kurt's natural voice, just not something to lean on as a crutch. Flagged for Kurt to confirm; if he wants it gone entirely, move it to the hard-no list.

## Listing structure preferences

Kurt's 8-step formula (in order):

1. **Lifestyle-driven opening paragraph** — set the feel before the facts.
2. **Location, atmosphere and setting** — where it is and what it feels like to arrive.
3. **Natural light and overall feel** of the home.
4. **Kitchen, living and entertaining** spaces — flow through the social heart of the home.
5. **Bedrooms, bathrooms and functionality** — how the home actually lives day to day.
6. **Outdoor spaces, sheds, parking and lifestyle extras.**
7. **Lifestyle benefits and location advantages** to finish the body.
8. **Clear call to action.**

### Word-count tiers

- **Standard homes:** 450–700 words.
- **Premium lifestyle / rural properties:** 700–1000 words.
- **Land listings:** 180–300 words.

These tiers override the workflow's generic ">500 words" guidance. Pick the tier that fits the property and state which you are targeting in the Phase 3 pre-flight plan.

## Market focus (context, not voice)

North West Coast of Tasmania — Ulverstone, Penguin, Turners Beach, Devonport and surrounds — across residential homes, family properties, coastal homes, lifestyle acreage, rural retreats and vacant land. Also lifestyle and holiday properties through the Central Highlands (Miena, Doctors Point, Tods Corner and surrounding highland communities). Use this to judge which buyer the lifestyle angle should speak to, not as copy to repeat.
