# Voice rules: Kurt Knowles

*Distilled from `knowledge/sample-listings/kurt-knowles__2026-05-28__CopyProBrandGuide.docx`. The brand guide has the long form; this file is the always / never / vocab quick reference Kurt's prompts check first.*

## Always

- Open with a lifestyle-driven paragraph: the feel, the setting, the pace, before any room-by-room detail.
- Describe the natural light and the overall feel of the home early.
- Balance every lifestyle line with a concrete, practical detail. Buyers should finish knowing how the home actually works.
- Run the 8-step formula in order (see brand-guide.md): lifestyle opener, location and setting, light and feel, kitchen/living/entertaining, beds/baths/functionality, outdoor and extras, lifestyle and location, CTA.
- Match the word-count tier to the property: 450–700 standard, 700–1000 premium/rural, 180–300 land.
- Keep it welcoming and easy to read while staying professional.
- Close with the exact CTA: "For more information or to book a private inspection please call the listing agent Kurt Knowles today!"

## Never

- Use "boasts", "well appointed", or "accommodation is well catered for".
- Use "cozy", "quaint", or "fixer-upper".
- Tip into corporate or sales-heavy wording.
- Overdo the emotion. No over-the-top, exaggerated or unrealistic descriptions. Restraint is the brand.
- Lean on hype to carry a line that has no practical substance underneath it.

## Vocabulary

- **Prefers:** light, bright and welcoming; calm and relaxed lifestyle; outdoor entertaining; sunshine streaming through; fabulous entertaining spaces; peaceful and private; lifestyle appeal; warm timber tones; fresh coastal feel; spacious and functional; easy living; coastal living; family friendly; natural light; slower pace; fresh air; "this home just works"; "buyers will absolutely love"; practical and well designed; relaxed modern feel; everyday living made easy.
- **Avoids:** boasts, well appointed, accommodation is well catered for, cozy, quaint, fixer-upper.
- **Use sparingly (source conflict):** "lovely" — the guide lists it to avoid, but the gold standards use it often. Use it naturally where it fits, do not lean on it. Pending Kurt's confirmation.
