# Voice rules: Kurt Knowles

*Distilled from `knowledge/sample-listings/kurt-knowles__2026-06-09__CopyProBrandGuide.docx`. The brand guide has the long form; this file is the always / never / vocab quick reference Kurt's prompts check first. **Updated 2026-06-09 — supersedes the prior "restraint over hype" voice.***

## Always

- Lead with a **strong attention-grabbing headline**. A short punchy phrase or a hooky question is the default ("Large Family Getaway or Perfect Air-BnB?", "What A Showstopper!!").
- Open the body with **the property's biggest single appeal**. Whatever makes this place remarkable goes in the first paragraph, before any room-by-room detail.
- Run the 7-step structure in order: headline, biggest-appeal opener, internal features and floorplan, lifestyle and entertaining, outdoor and sheds, location and amenities, CTA.
- Write **as if personally showing a buyer through the property**. Conversational, warm, easy to read. Sound like Kurt, not like a brochure.
- Be **enthusiastic and excited about the property**. Use exclamation marks where the line genuinely earns the energy — single exclamation per paragraph in the body, double-exclamation allowed in headlines if it suits.
- Frame **lifestyle, family living and entertaining** as the spine — practicality and location benefits ride alongside.
- Match the word-count tier to the property: **450–700 standard, 700–1000 premium/rural, 250–300 land.**
- Close with the **exact CTA**: "For more information or to book a private inspection please contact the listing agent Kurt Knowles today!"

## Never

- Use **corporate language** (KPI-speak, business-school vocab, "value proposition").
- Use **overly technical descriptions** that read like a building inspection report.
- Use **investor jargon** (yields, gross returns, cap rates). Kurt's buyers are families, lifestyle changers and shack owners.
- Drop **generic real estate clichés without context** ("this property has it all", "must be seen to be appreciated") unless the surrounding sentences prove the claim.
- Use **excessive bullet-point writing** in the body. Paragraphs are the format. Bullets belong in the Window Card and RealEstateVIEW Guide.
- Sound **formal or robotic**. If a sentence reads stiff, rewrite it warmer.
- Chain exclamation marks inside body copy or stack multiple per sentence — the Rulebook's "no repeated exclamation marks" still applies; one per paragraph max, only when earned.

## Vocabulary

- **Prefers (from the brand guide's explicit list):**
  - Fantastic
  - Amazing
  - Stunning
  - Gorgeous
  - Beautiful
  - Luxurious
  - Spacious
  - Sun-drenched
  - Perfect for
  - Could not be better positioned
  - The perfect place to entertain
  - Larger family
  - Practicality
  - Lifestyle
  - Privacy and practicality
  - Rare opportunity
  - Highly sought after
  - World-class

- **Avoids (category-level, not word-level):** corporate language, overly technical descriptions, investor jargon, generic clichés without context, excessive bullet-point style writing, anything that sounds formal or robotic.

- **No specific word bans this time.** The May 28 hard-no list ("boasts", "well appointed", "cozy", "quaint", "fixer-upper") is no longer in force — the new brand guide doesn't name them. Judge each on whether it fits Kurt's natural enthusiasm; replace anything that lands as corporate or sales-heavy in the moment, not by reflex.
