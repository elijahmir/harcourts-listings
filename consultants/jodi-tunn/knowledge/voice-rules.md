# Voice rules: Jodi Tunn

*Distilled from `knowledge/sample-listings/jodi-tunn__2026-06-02__CopyProBrandGuide.docx`. The brand guide has the long form; this file is the always / never / vocab quick reference Jodi's prompts check first.*

## Always

- Lead with **lifestyle**, not features. Open with the family rhythm and feel of the home before any room-by-room detail.
- Run the prescribed 5-section structure in order, respecting the per-section word budgets: headline (5–10), lifestyle opening (75–100), home and living spaces (100–150), bedrooms / flexibility / comfort (75–100), location and lifestyle close (50–75). Total ~350–450 words.
- Frame the home around **flexibility and future possibilities**: how it adapts to growing families, extended living, dual-living, downsizers, or work-from-home.
- Tell each space room by room. Show how it's actually used. The master suite is a "true retreat"; the storage room is "ideal for bikes, a home gym or hobby space".
- Call out the **practical features** that matter for everyday liveability: off-street parking for boats / caravans, solar panels, automated watering, workshops, storage.
- Close with **community and coastal lifestyle**, not just a distance list. Mention sporting precincts, schools, the North West Coast feel.
- For Team Tunn listings, the CTA names both: "Call Colin or Jodi to book a private inspection."
- Keep it warm, inviting and believable. Authentic, Trusted, Caring.

## Never

- Use any of these high-pressure / cliché phrases (Jodi's explicit ban list): "This property won't last long", "Be quick", "Act now", "Motivated vendor", "Must be sold", "Priced to sell", "Hot property", "Investor's dream", "Hidden gem", "Location, location, location", "Opportunity knocks", "Luxury living at its finest", "One not to miss", "Sure to impress", "Ticks all the boxes".
- Pitch the home like a high-pressure auction ad. No urgency-baiting, no "missing out" framing, no investor-pitch energy.
- Over-polish the prose into something cold and corporate. Warmth wins.
- Lead with features over feeling. Open the door with the lifestyle first.
- Skip the human angle. Jodi sells homes through the people who will live in them.

## Vocabulary

- **Prefers:** lifestyle-focused; flexible family living; space, privacy and convenience; sweeping views and outlooks; versatility and opportunity; room to grow; the heart of the home; indoor-outdoor living; emotional connection; community and coastal lifestyle; true retreat; everyday liveability.
- **Avoids:** see the full hard-no list above. In one line: anything that smells like high-pressure sales talk.
