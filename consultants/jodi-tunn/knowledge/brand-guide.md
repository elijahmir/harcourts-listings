# Brand guide: Jodi Tunn

*Distilled from `knowledge/sample-listings/jodi-tunn__2026-06-02__CopyProBrandGuide.docx` (Jodi Tunn CopyPro Brand Guide). The docx is the citation and holds her three gold-standard listings; this file is what every session reads first.*

## Voice identity

**Authentic. Trusted. Caring.** Jodi is professional enough to be trusted, genuine enough to feel like a friend, and experienced enough to guide people through life's biggest property decisions with confidence. The writing is **relationship-first**: it speaks to the people behind the property, not just the property itself. People first, property second. Warm and inviting, never overly polished. Family-oriented and aspirational, but always believable.

## Tone

- **Formal to casual:** warm and approachable, never stiff. Reads like a trusted friend who happens to know real estate inside out, never like a high-pressure salesperson.
- **Warm to professional:** care first, with quiet professional polish underneath. Strong local knowledge sits inside friendly, conversational sentences.
- **Aspirational to grounded:** paints a real lifestyle the buyer can picture themselves stepping into. Aspirational, yes, but always honest and believable.

## Signature patterns

- **Lifestyle-led opening, not feature-led.** Set the feel and the family rhythm of the home before any room-by-room detail.
- **"Flexibility and future possibilities" angle.** Jodi's homes are described in terms of how they adapt to growing families, extended living, dual-living, downsizers, work-from-home. The property's potential is part of the pitch.
- **Detailed room-by-room storytelling.** Each section paints how a space is actually used (the master suite as a "true retreat", the storage room "ideal for bikes, a home gym or hobby space").
- **Practical features matter.** Off-street parking for a boat or caravan, solar panels, automated watering systems, workshop areas. These earn explicit mentions because Jodi's buyers care about everyday liveability.
- **Community and coastal close.** End with how the location actually feels to live in (sporting precincts, local school, North West Coast lifestyle), not just a list of distances.
- **CTA pairs with Colin Tunn when relevant.** Jodi and Colin work as Team Tunn. When appropriate the closing CTA names both ("Call Colin or Jodi to book a private inspection.").

## Hard nos (beyond the No-Robot Rulebook)

Jodi has the most explicit "do not use" list of any consultant. Every one of these is a high-pressure or cliché real-estate phrase that breaks the warm, honest tone:

- "This property won't last long"
- "Be quick"
- "Act now"
- "Motivated vendor"
- "Must be sold"
- "Priced to sell"
- "Hot property"
- "Investor's dream"
- "Hidden gem"
- "Location, location, location"
- "Opportunity knocks"
- "Luxury living at its finest"
- "One not to miss"
- "Sure to impress"
- "Ticks all the boxes"

If a sentence sounds like it belongs in a high-pressure auction ad, it does not belong in a Jodi listing.

## Listing structure preferences

Jodi's prescribed structure with per-section word budgets:

1. **Headline** — 5–10 words.
2. **Lifestyle opening** — 75–100 words. Set the family rhythm, the feel, the kind of life this home enables.
3. **Home and living spaces** — 100–150 words. Walk through kitchen, living, dining, outdoor entertaining; how the home flows.
4. **Bedrooms, flexibility and comfort** — 75–100 words. Master suite as a retreat; additional bedrooms; dual-living, extended-family, or WFH possibilities.
5. **Location and lifestyle close** — 50–75 words. The community, the coast, the actual feel of living there. Plus the CTA (often "Call Colin or Jodi…" for Team Tunn listings).

**Total target: ~350–450 words** for the body.

> **Note on workflow defaults.** The 5-phase workflow's generic guidance asks for ">500 words" in Phase 3. Jodi's brand voice wins (per the consultant CLAUDE.md non-negotiables): target her 350–450 word total. State which tier you are targeting in the Phase 3 pre-flight plan.

## Market focus (context, not copy to repeat)

Penguin and the North West Coast of Tasmania. Residential property sales with particular strength in **family homes** and homes offering **flexible or dual-living** options. Jodi's true niche is people, not a property type. She is especially trusted by **families, long-term locals, downsizers, and clients who value relationships and a hands-on approach over a purely transactional one**. Calibrate listings to those buyers' priorities (lifestyle, flexibility, community, long-term liveability), never to a "be quick or miss out" investor pitch.
